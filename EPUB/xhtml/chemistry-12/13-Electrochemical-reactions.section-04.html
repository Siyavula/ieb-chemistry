Processes in electrochemical cells

Half-cells and half-reactions

Galvanic cells are actually made up of two half-cells. One half-cell contains the anode and an electrolyte containing the same metal cations. The other half-cell contains the cathode and an electrolyte containing the same metal cations. These half-cells are connected by a salt-bridge and the electrodes are connected through an external circuit.

Earlier in this chapter we discussed a zinc-copper cell. This was made up of a zinc half cell, containing a zinc electrode and a zinc sulphate (CLICKME\text{ZnSO}_{4}) electrolyte solution, and a copper half cell, containing a copper electrode and a copper sulphate solution.

Half cell

A half cell is a structure that consists of a conductive electrode surrounded by a conductive electrolyte.

In each half-cell a half-reaction takes place:

  • Copper plate

    At the copper plate, there was an increase in mass. This means that CLICKME\text{Cu}^{2+} ions from the copper sulfate solution were deposited onto the plate as atoms of copper metal. The half-reaction that takes place at the copper plate is:

    CLICKME\text{Cu}^{2+}(\text{aq}) + 2\text{e}^{-}CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Cu}(\text{s})

    As electrons are gained by the copper ions this is the CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\textbf{reduction half-reaction}}.

  • Zinc plate

    At the zinc plate, there was a decrease in mass. This means that some of the solid zinc goes into solution as CLICKME\text{Zn}^{2+} ions. The electrons remain on the zinc plate, giving it a negative charge. The half-reaction that takes place at the zinc plate is:

    CLICKME\text{Zn}(\text{s})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Zn}^{2+}(\text{aq}) + 2\text{e}^{-}

    As electrons are lost by the zinc atoms this is the CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\textbf{oxidation half-reaction}}.

  • The overall reaction

    You can then combine the two half-reactions from these two half-cells to get the overall reaction:

    CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\text{Zn(s)}} + \textcolor{red}{\text{Cu}^{2+}\text{(aq)}} + \textcolor{red}{\text{2e}^{-}} \to \textcolor{blue}{\text{Zn}^{2+}\text{(aq)}} + \textcolor{red}{\text{Cu(s)}} + \textcolor{blue}{\text{2e}^{-}} or, if we cancel the electrons:

    CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\text{Zn(s)}} + \textcolor{red}{\text{Cu}^{2+}\text{(aq)}} \to \textcolor{blue}{\text{Zn}^{2+}\text{(aq)}} + \textcolor{red}{\text{Cu(s)}}

It is possible to look at the half-reaction taking place in a half-cell and determine which electrode is the anode and which is the cathode.

  • CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\textbf{Oxidation}} is loss at the CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\textbf{anode}}, therefore the oxidation half-reaction occurs in the half-cell containing the anode.

  • CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\textbf{Reduction}} is gain at the CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\textbf{cathode}} so the reduction half-reaction occurs in the half-cell containing the cathode.

This means that for this electrochemical cell, the cell standard notation is:

CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\textbf{Zn(s)}}CLICKME|CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\textbf{Zn}^{2+}\textbf{(aq)}}CLICKME||CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\textbf{Cu}^{2+}\textbf{(aq)}}CLICKME|CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\textbf{Cu(s)}}

CLICKME| = a phase boundary (solid/aqueous)

CLICKME|| = the salt bridge

Understanding galvanic cells

For the following cell:

CLICKME\text{Zn}(\text{s})|\text{Zn}^{2+}(\text{aq})||\text{Ag}^{+}(\text{aq})|\text{Ag}(\text{s})

  1. Give the anode and cathode half-reactions.

  2. Write the overall equation for the chemical reaction.

  3. Give the direction of the current in the external circuit.

Identify the oxidation and reduction reactions

In standard cell notation, the anode is written on the left and the cathode is written on the right. So, in this cell:

Zinc is the anode (solid zinc is oxidised).

Silver is the cathode (silver ions are reduced).

Write the two half-reactions

Anode: CLICKME\text{Zn}(\text{s})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Zn}^{2+}(\text{aq}) + 2\text{e}^{-}

Cathode: CLICKME\text{Ag}^{+}(\text{aq}) + \text{e}^{-}CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Ag}(\text{s})

Combine the half-reactions to get the overall equation.

Balance the charge by multiplying the reduction half-reaction by CLICKME\text{2}.

CLICKME2\text{Ag}^{+}(\text{aq}) + 2\text{e}^{-}CLICKME\toCLICKME2\text{Ag}(\text{s})

CLICKME\text{Zn}(\text{s}) + 2\text{Ag}^{+}(\text{aq})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Zn}^{2+}(\text{aq}) + \text{Ag}(\text{s})

Determine the direction of current flow

The solid zinc is oxidised to form zinc ions. These electrons are left on the zinc electrode (anode) making it negative.

The silver ions take electrons and are reduced to form solid silver. This makes the silver electrode (cathode) positive.

Electron flow is from negative to positive, so from the anode to the cathode. Conventional current is in the opposite direction to electron flow. Therefore current will flow from the cathode (silver) to the anode (zinc).

Predictions in half-cells

Reactions at the anode and cathode

A cell contains a silver anode and a copper cathode. Give the half-cell reactions occuring at the anode and cathode, as well as standard cell notation for this cell.

What type of reaction occurs at the anode, and what type occurs at the cathode?

Oxidation is loss of electrons at the anode. So the silver anode will be oxidised.

Reduction is gain of electrons at the cathode. So the copper cathode will be reduced.

Write down the half-reactions as they would occur in the cell

CLICKME\text{Ag}(\text{s})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Ag}^{+}(\text{aq}) + \text{e}^{-} (oxidation half-reaction)

CLICKME\text{Cu}^{2+}(\text{aq}) + 2\text{e}^{-}CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Cu}(\text{s}) (reduction half-reaction)

Give the standard cell notation for this reaction

The anode is always written first (on the left): CLICKME\text{Ag}(\text{s})|\text{Ag}^{+}(\text{aq})

The cathode is always written second (on the right): CLICKME\text{Cu}^{2+}(\text{aq})|\text{Cu}(\text{s})

Therefore the standard cell notation is:

CLICKME\text{Ag}(\text{s})|\text{Ag}^{+}(\text{aq})||\text{Cu}^{2+}(\text{aq})|\text{Cu}(\text{s})
