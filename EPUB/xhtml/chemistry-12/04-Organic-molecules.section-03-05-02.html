Naming esters

When an alcohol reacts with a carboxylic acid an ester is formed. A new bond is formed between the oxygen atom of the hydroxyl group and the carbon atom of the carboxylic acid. The suffix for an ester is -oate.

Note that although the part of the ester from the alcohol (giving the prefix) is on the left in Figure fig-organic-ethylbutanoate2 (a) in the condensed structural formula the part from the alcohol is on the right. Don't forget to count the carbon atom in the carbonyl group when determining the number of carbon atoms in the chain.
