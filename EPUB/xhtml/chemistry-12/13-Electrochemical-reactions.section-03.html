Writing redox and half-reactions

Redox reactions and half-reactions

Remember from Grade 11 that oxidation and reduction occur simultaneously in a redox reaction. The reactions taking place in electrochemical cells are redox reactions. That is:

  • Is there a compound or atom being oxidised?

  • Is there a compound or atom being reduced?

For example, the reaction given below is a redox reaction:

CLICKME2\text{Fe}^{3+}(\text{aq}) + \text{Sn}^{2+}(\text{aq})CLICKME\toCLICKME2\text{Fe}^{2+}(\text{aq}) + \text{Sn}^{4+}(\text{aq})

CLICKME\text{Fe}^{3+} is gaining an electron to become CLICKME\text{Fe}^{2+}. CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\textbf{Iron}} is therefore being CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\textbf{reduced}} and CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\textbf{tin}} is the CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\textbf{reducing agent}} (causing iron to be reduced). The CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\textbf{reduction half-reaction}} is:

CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\textbf{Fe}^{3+}\textbf{(aq) + e}^{-} \to \textbf{Fe}^{2+}\textbf{(aq)}}

CLICKME\text{Sn}^{2+} is losing two electrons to become CLICKME\text{Sn}^{4+}. CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\textbf{Tin}} is therefore being CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\textbf{oxidised}} and CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\textbf{iron}} is the CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\textbf{oxidising agent}} (causing tin to be oxidised). The CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\textbf{oxidation half-reaction}} is:

CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\textbf{Sn}^{2+}\textbf{(aq)} \to \textbf{Sn}^{4+}\textbf{(aq) + 2e}^{-}}

Notice that in the overall reaction the reduction half-reaction is multiplied by two. This is so that the number of electrons gained in the reduction half-reaction match the number of electrons lost in the oxidation half-reaction.

Balancing redox reactions

Half-reactions can be used to balance redox reactions. We are going to use some worked examples to help explain the method.

Balancing redox reactions

Chlorine gas oxidises CLICKME\text{Fe}^{2+} ions to CLICKME\text{Fe}^{3+} ions. In the process, chlorine is reduced to chloride ions. Write a balanced equation for this reaction.

Write down the unbalanced oxidation half-reaction.

CLICKME\text{Fe}^{2+}(\text{aq})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Fe}^{3+}(\text{aq})

Balance the number of atoms on both sides of the equation

There is one iron atom on the left and one on the right, so no additional atoms need to be added.

Once the atoms are balanced, check that the charges balance

The charge on the left of the equation is CLICKME\text{+2}, but the charge on the right is CLICKME\text{+3}. Therefore, one electron must be added to the right hand side so that the charges balance. The half-reaction is now:

CLICKME\text{Fe}^{2+}(\text{aq})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Fe}^{3+}(\text{aq}) + \text{e}^{-}

Repeat steps CLICKME\text{1} - CLICKME\text{3} with the reduction half-reaction

The unbalanced reduction half-reaction is:

CLICKME\text{Cl}_{2}(\text{g})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Cl}^{-}(\text{aq})

The atoms don't balance, so we need to multiply the right hand side by two to fix this.

CLICKME\text{Cl}_{2}(\text{g})CLICKME\toCLICKME2\text{Cl}^{-}(\text{aq})

Two electrons must be added to the left hand side to balance the charges.

CLICKME\text{Cl}_{2}(\text{g}) + 2\text{e}^{-}CLICKME\toCLICKME2\text{Cl}^{-}(\text{aq})

Compare the number of electrons in each equation

Multiply each half-reaction by a suitable number so that the number of electrons released (oxidation) is equal to the number of electrons accepted (reduction).

oxidation half-reaction: CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\times \textbf{2}}: CLICKME\textcolor{red}{2}CLICKME\text{Fe}^{2+}(\text{aq})CLICKME\toCLICKME\textcolor{red}{2}CLICKME\text{Fe}^{3+}(\text{aq}) +CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\textbf{2}}{\textbf{e}^{-}}

reduction half-reaction: CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\times \textbf{1}}: CLICKME\text{Cl}_{2}(\text{g}) +CLICKME\textbf{2e}^{-} \toCLICKME2\text{Cl}^{-}(\text{aq})

Combine the two half-reactions to get a final equation for the overall reaction

CLICKME2\text{Fe}^{2+}(\text{aq}) + \text{Cl}_{2}(\text{g})CLICKME\toCLICKME2\text{Fe}^{3+}(\text{aq}) + 2\text{Cl}^{-}(\text{aq})

Do a final check to make sure that the equation is balanced

We check the number of atoms and the charges and find that the equation is balanced.

Balancing redox reactions in an acid medium

The following reaction takes place in an acid medium:

CLICKME\text{Cr}_{2}\text{O}_{7}^{2-}(\text{aq}) + \text{H}_{2}\text{S}(\text{g})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Cr}^{3+}(\text{aq}) + \text{S}(\text{s})

Write a balanced equation for this reaction.

Write down the unbalanced reduction half-reaction

In CLICKME\text{Cr}_{2}\text{O}_{7}^{2-} chromium exists as CLICKME\text{Cr}^{6+}. It becomes CLICKME\text{Cr}^{3+}. Therefore electrons are gained, this is the reduction half-reaction:

CLICKME\text{Cr}_{2}\text{O}_{7}^{2-}(\text{aq})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Cr}^{3+}(\text{aq})

Balance the number of atoms on both sides of the equation

We need to multiply the right side by two so that the number of Cr atoms will balance. To balance the oxygen atoms, we will need to add water molecules to the right hand side.

CLICKME\text{Cr}_{2}\text{O}_{7}^{2-}(\text{aq})CLICKME\toCLICKME2\text{Cr}^{3+}(\text{aq}) + 7\text{H}_{2}\text{O}(\text{ℓ})

Now the oxygen atoms balance but the hydrogens don't. Because the reaction takes place in an acid medium, we can add hydrogen ions to the left side.

CLICKME\text{Cr}_{2}\text{O}_{7}^{2-}(\text{aq}) + 14\text{H}^{+}(\text{aq})CLICKME\toCLICKME2\text{Cr}^{3+}(\text{aq}) + 7\text{H}_{2}\text{O}(\text{ℓ})

Once the atoms are balanced, check that the charges balance

The charge on the left of the equation is (CLICKME-\text{2} + CLICKME\text{14}) = CLICKME\text{+12}, but the charge on the right is CLICKME\text{+6}. Therefore, six electrons must be added to the left hand side so that the charges balance. This makes sense as electrons are gained in the reduction half-reaction. The half-reaction is now:

CLICKME\text{Cr}_{2}\text{O}_{7}^{2-}(\text{aq}) + 14\text{H}^{+}(\text{aq}) + 6\text{e}^{-}CLICKME\toCLICKME2\text{Cr}^{3+}(\text{aq}) + 7\text{H}_{2}\text{O}(\text{ℓ})

Repeat steps CLICKME\text{1} - CLICKME\text{3} with the oxidation half-reaction

CLICKME\text{H}_{2}\text{S}(\text{g})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{S}(\text{s})

However, you can ignore the CLICKME\text{H}^{+} as they are accounted for by the acid medium and the reduction half-reaction.

CLICKME\text{S}^{2-}(\text{aq})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{S}(\text{s})

The atoms balance, however the charges do not. Two electrons must be added to the right hand side of the equation.

CLICKME\text{S}^{2-}(\text{aq})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{S}(\text{s}) + 2\text{e}^{-}

Compare the number of electrons in each equation

Multiply each half-reaction by a suitable number so that the number of electrons released (oxidation) is equal to the number of electrons accepted (reduction).

oxidation half-reaction: CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\times \textbf{3}}: CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\text{3}}CLICKME\text{S}^{2-}(\text{aq})CLICKME\toCLICKME\textcolor{red}{\text{3}}CLICKME\text{S}(\text{s}) +CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\textbf{6}}{\textbf{e}^{-}}

reduction half-reaction: CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\times \textbf{1}}: CLICKME\text{Cr}_{2}\text{O}_{7}^{2-}(\text{aq}) + 14\text{H}^{+}(\text{aq}) +CLICKME\textbf{6e}^{-} \toCLICKME2\text{Cr}^{3+}(\text{aq}) + 7\text{H}_{2}\text{O}(\text{ℓ})

Combine the two half-reactions to get a final equation for the overall reaction

CLICKME\text{Cr}_{2}\text{O}_{7}^{2-}(\text{aq}) + 14\text{H}^{+}(\text{aq}) + 3\text{S}^{2-}(\text{aq})CLICKME\toCLICKME3\text{S}(\text{s}) + 2\text{Cr}^{3+}(\text{aq}) + 7\text{H}_{2}\text{O}(\text{ℓ})

CLICKME\text{Cr}_{2}\text{O}_{7}^{2-}(\text{aq}) + 8\text{H}^{+}(\text{aq}) + 3\text{H}_{2}\text{S}(\text{g})CLICKME\toCLICKME3\text{S}(\text{s}) + 2\text{Cr}^{3+}(\text{aq}) + 7\text{H}_{2}\text{O}(\text{ℓ}) would also be acceptable.

Do a final check to make sure that the equation is balanced

We check the number of atoms and the charges and find that the equation is balanced.

Balancing redox reactions in an alkaline medium

The complex ion hexaaminecobalt(II) (CLICKME\text{Co}(\text{NH}_{3})_{6}^{2+}) is oxidised by hydrogen peroxide to form the hexaaminecobalt(III) ion (CLICKME\text{Co}(\text{NH}_{3})_{6}^{3+}). Write a balanced equation for this reaction.

Write down the unbalanced oxidation half-reaction

In CLICKME\text{Co}(\text{NH}_{3})_{6}^{2+} cobalt exists as CLICKME\text{Co}^{2+}. In CLICKME\text{Co}(\text{NH}_{3})_{6}^{3+} cobalt exists as CLICKME\text{Co}^{3+}. Therefore electrons are lost, this is the oxidation half-reaction:

CLICKME\text{Co}(\text{NH}_{3})_{6}^{2+}(\text{aq})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Co}(\text{NH}_{3})_{6}^{3+}(\text{aq})

Balance the number of atoms on both sides of the equation.

The number of atoms are the same on both sides.

Once the atoms are balanced, check that the charges balance

The charge on the left of the equation is CLICKME\text{+2}, but the charge on the right is CLICKME\text{+3}. One electron must be added to the right hand side to balance the charges in the equation:

CLICKME\text{Co}(\text{NH}_{3})_{6}^{2+}CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Co}(\text{NH}_{3})_{6}^{3+} + \text{e}^{-}

Repeat steps CLICKME\text{1} - CLICKME\text{3} with the reduction half-reaction

The most logical product for the reduction of hydrogen peroxide is CLICKME\text{OH}^{-}:

CLICKME\text{H}_{2}\text{O}_{2}(\text{ℓ})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{OH}^{-}(\text{aq})

Next you need to balance the atoms:

CLICKME\text{H}_{2}\text{O}_{2}(\text{ℓ})CLICKME\toCLICKME2\text{OH}^{-}(\text{aq})

Then you need to balance the charges:

CLICKME\text{H}_{2}\text{O}_{2}(\text{ℓ}) + 2\text{e}^{-}CLICKME\toCLICKME2\text{OH}^{-}(\text{aq})

Compare the number of electrons in each equation

Multiply each half-reaction by a suitable number so that the number of electrons released (oxidation) is equal to the number of electrons accepted (reduction):

oxidation half-reaction: CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\times \textbf{2}}: CLICKME\textcolor{red}{2}CLICKME\text{Co}(\text{NH}_{3})_{6}^{2+}(\text{aq})CLICKME\to \textcolor{red}{2}CLICKME\text{Co}(\text{NH}_{3})_{6}^{3+}(\text{aq}) +CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\textbf{2}}{\textbf{e}}^{-}

reduction half-reaction: CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\times \textbf{1}}: CLICKME\text{H}_{2}\text{O}_{2}(\text{ℓ}) +CLICKME\textbf{2e}^{-} \toCLICKME2\text{OH}^{-}(\text{aq})

Combine the two half-reactions to get a final equation for the overall reaction

CLICKME2\text{Co}(\text{NH}_{3})_{6}^{2+}(\text{aq}) + \text{H}_{2}\text{O}_{2}(\text{ℓ})CLICKME\toCLICKME2\text{Co}(\text{NH}_{3})_{6}^{3+}(\text{aq}) + 2\text{OH}^{-}(\text{aq})

Do a final check to make sure that the equation is balanced

We check the number of atoms and the charges and find that the equation is balanced.

CLICKME\text{Fe}(\text{s})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Fe}^{2+}(\text{aq}) + 2\text{e}^{-} (oxidation is loss of electrons at the anode).
