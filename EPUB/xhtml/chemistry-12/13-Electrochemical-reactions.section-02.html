The effects of current and potential on rate and equilibrium

Let's think back to the CLICKME\text{Zn}-CLICKME\text{Cu}(\text{s}) electrochemical cell. This cell is made up of two half cells and the reactions that take place at each of the electrodes are as follows:

CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\text{Zn(s)} \to \text{Zn}^{2+}\text{(aq) + 2e}^{-}}

CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\text{Cu}^{2+}\text{(aq) + 2e}^{-} \to \text{Cu(s)}}

Current and rate of reaction

At the zinc electrode, the zinc metal loses electrons and forms CLICKME\text{Zn}^{2+} ions. The electrons are concentrated on the zinc metal while the CLICKME\text{Zn}^{2+} ions are in solution.

At the copper electrode, the copper ions gain electrons and form solid copper. This means that there are less electrons built up on the copper electrode.

The electrons left by the zinc metal on the zinc electrode then flow through the external circuit towards the more positive copper electrode. Therefore the faster the chemical reactions take place (CLICKME\text{Zn}(\text{s})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Zn}^{2+}(\text{aq}) + 2\text{e}^{-} and CLICKME\text{Cu}^{2+}(\text{aq}) + 2\text{e}^{-}CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Cu}(\text{s})) the faster electrons will flow through the external circuit and the greater the current will be. Remember that the Zn-Cu cell is a galvanic cell.

Conversely, in an electrolytic cell a redox reaction takes place when a current is applied. This redox reaction is the decomposition of a chemical compound (electrolysis). The rate of this decomposition (into ions) is increased when the current applied is increased.

Potential difference, equilibrium and concentration

As the zinc-copper cell reaction proceeds:

  • There will be less and less solid zinc and more and more zinc ions

    Eventually the amount of solid zinc atoms losing electrons and becoming zinc ions will equal the amount of zinc ions gaining electrons and becoming solid zinc atoms:

    CLICKME\text{Zn}(\text{s})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Zn}^{2+}(\text{aq}) + 2\text{e}^{-}

  • There will be more and more solid copper and less and less copper ions

    Eventually the amount of copper ions gaining electrons and becoming solid copper atoms will equal the amount of solid copper atoms losing electrons and becoming copper ions:

When equilibrium is reached and the amount of reactants and products becomes constant the potential difference of this cell will be CLICKME\text{0}. There will be no excess electrons on the zinc electrode and no deficit of electrons on the copper electrode:

  • The electrons lost by CLICKME\text{Zn}(\text{s}) becoming CLICKME\text{Zn}^{2+}(\text{aq}) will be gained by the CLICKME\text{Zn}^{2+}(\text{aq}) becoming CLICKME\text{Zn}(\text{s}) as at equilibrium the rate of the forward and reverse reactions are equal.

  • The CLICKME\text{Cu}^{2+}(\text{aq}) will not need electrons from the external circuit as the rate of CLICKME\text{Cu}(\text{s})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Cu}^{2+}(\text{aq}) +2\text{e}^{-} equals the rate of CLICKME\text{Cu}^{2+}(\text{aq}) + 2\text{e}^{-}CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{Cu}(\text{s}).

So the potential difference across a cell is related to the extent to which the cell reaction has reached equilibrium. In other words, as the reaction proceeds and the concentration of reactants decreases and the concentration of products increases, the reaction approaches equilibrium. When equilibrium is reached, the potential difference of the cell is zero and the cell is said to be 'flat'. There is no longer a potential difference between the two half cells, and no more current will flow.

A positive potential difference means that the forward reaction is spontaneous while a negative potential difference means that the forward reaction is non-spontaneous, and that the reverse reaction is favoured. This will be discussed in more detail later in this chapter.
