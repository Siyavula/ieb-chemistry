Conjugate acid-base pairs

Look at the reaction between hydrochloric acid and ammonia to form ammonium and chloride ions:

HCl ( aq ) + NH 3 ( g ) \text{HCl}(\text{aq}) + \text{NH}_{3}(\text{g}) ⇋ ⇋ NH 4 + ( aq ) + Cl − ( aq ) \text{NH}_{4}^{+}(\text{aq}) + \text{Cl}^{-}(\text{aq})

Looking at the forward reaction (i.e. the reaction that proceeds from left to right), the changes that take place can be shown as follows:

  • \textcolor r e d HCl(aq) → Cl − (aq) + \textcolor d a r k g r e e n H + (aq) {\textcolor{red}{\text{HCl(aq)}}} \to {\text{Cl}}^{-}{\text{(aq)}} + {\textcolor{darkgreen}{{\text{H}}^{+}{\text{(aq)}}}}

  • \textcolor b l u e NH 3 (g) + \textcolor d a r k g r e e n H + (aq) → NH 4 + (aq) {\textcolor{blue}{\text{NH}_{3}{\text{(g)}}}} + {\textcolor{darkgreen}{\text{H}^{+}{\text{(aq)}}}} \to {\text{NH}_{4}^{+}{\text{(aq)}}}

  • In the forward reaction, HCl is a proton donor ( \textcolor r e d acid {\textcolor{red}{\textbf{acid}}}) and NH 3 \text{NH}_{3} is a proton acceptor ( \textcolor b l u e base {\textcolor{blue}{\textbf{base}}}).

Looking at the reverse reaction (i.e. the reaction that proceeds from right to left), the changes that take place are as follows:

  • \textcolor b l u e Cl − (aq) + \textcolor d a r k g r e e n H + (aq) → HCl(aq) {\textcolor{blue}{\text{Cl}^{-}{\text{(aq)}}}} + {\textcolor{darkgreen}{{\text{H}}^{+}{\text{(aq)}}}} \to {\text{HCl(aq)}}

  • \textcolor r e d NH 4 + (aq) → NH 3 (g) + \textcolor d a r k g r e e n H + (aq) {\textcolor{red}{\text{NH}_{4}^{+}{\text{(aq)}}}} \to {\text{NH}_{3}{\text{(g)}}} + {\textcolor{darkgreen}{{\text{H}}^{+}{\text{(aq)}}}}

  • In the reverse reaction, the chloride ion ( Cl − \text{Cl}^{-}) is the proton acceptor ( \textcolor b l u e base {\textcolor{blue}{\textbf{base}}}) and the ammonium ion ( NH 4 + \text{NH}_{4}^{+}) is the proton donor ( \textcolor r e d acid {\textcolor{red}{\textbf{acid}}}).

In the forward reaction \textcolor r e d HCl \textcolor{red}{\text{HCl}} donates a proton ( H + \text{H}^{+}) to form \textcolor b l u e Cl − \textcolor{blue}{\text{Cl}^{-}}. In the reverse reaction \textcolor b l u e Cl − \textcolor{blue}{\text{Cl}^{-}} accepts a proton to form \textcolor r e d HCl \textcolor{red}{\text{HCl}}. \textcolor b l u e Cl − \textcolor{blue}{\text{Cl}^{-}} is the \textcolor b l u e conjugate base \textcolor{blue}{\text{conjugate base}} of the \textcolor r e d acid HCl \textcolor{red}{\text{acid HCl}}. So HCl \text{HCl} and Cl − \text{Cl}^{-} are a \newrgbcolor o r a n g e 1 0.27 0 \textcolor o r a n g e conjugate acid-base pair \newrgbcolor{orange}{1 0.27 0}{\textcolor{orange}{\textbf{conjugate acid-base pair}}}.

Similarly, in the forward reaction \textcolor b l u e NH 3 \textcolor{blue}{\text{NH}_{3}} accepts a proton ( H + \text{H}^{+}) to form \textcolor r e d NH 4 + \textcolor{red}{{\text{NH}}_{4}^{+}}. In the reverse reaction \textcolor r e d NH 4 + \textcolor{red}{{\text{NH}}_{4}^{+}} donates a proton to form \textcolor b l u e NH 3 \textcolor{blue}{\text{NH}_{3}}. \textcolor r e d NH 4 + \textcolor{red}{\text{NH}_{4}^{+}} is the \textcolor r e d conjugate acid \textcolor{red}{\text{conjugate acid}} of the \textcolor b l u e base NH 3 \textcolor{blue}{\text{base NH}_{3}}. So NH 3 \text{NH}_{3} and NH 4 + \text{NH}_{4}^{+} are a \newrgbcolor p u r p l e 0.78 0.13 1 \textcolor p u r p l e conjugate acid-base \newrgbcolor{purple}{0.78 0.13 1}{\textcolor{purple}{\textbf{conjugate acid-base}}} \textcolor p u r p l e pair \textcolor{purple}{\textbf{pair}}.

Conjugate acid-base pair

A conjugate acid-base pair contains two compounds that differ only by a hydrogen ion ( H + \text{H}^{+}) and a charge of +1 \text{+1}.

Determine the conjugate acid-base pairs for the following reaction:

HNO 3 ( aq ) + OH − ( aq ) \text{HNO}_{3}(\text{aq}) + \text{OH}^{-}(\text{aq}) ⇌ \rightleftharpoons NO 3 − ( aq ) + H 2 O ( ℓ ) \text{NO}_{3}^{-}(\text{aq}) + \text{H}_{2}\text{O}(\text{ℓ})

Which reactant is an acid and which is a base?

HNO 3 \text{HNO}_{3} is nitric acid. It donates a proton in the forward reaction:

HNO 3 ( aq ) \text{HNO}_{3}(\text{aq}) → \to NO 3 − ( aq ) + H + ( aq ) \text{NO}_{3}^{-}(\text{aq}) + \text{H}^{+}(\text{aq})

OH − \text{OH}^{-} accepts a proton in the forward reaction and is therefore the base:

OH − ( aq ) + H + ( aq ) \text{OH}^{-}(\text{aq}) + \text{H}^{+}(\text{aq}) → \to H 2 O ( ℓ ) \text{H}_{2}\text{O}(\text{ℓ})

Which product is the conjugate base of the acid?

Nitric acid ( HNO 3 \text{HNO}_{3}) donates a proton to become NO 3 − \text{NO}_{3}^{-}. In the reverse reaction NO 3 − \text{NO}_{3}^{-} accepts a proton to become HNO 3 \text{HNO}_{3}. Therefore NO 3 − \text{NO}_{3}^{-} is the conjugate base of HNO 3 \text{HNO}_{3}.

NO 3 − ( aq ) + H + ( aq ) \text{NO}_{3}^{-}(\text{aq}) + \text{H}^{+}(\text{aq}) → \to HNO 3 ( aq ) \text{HNO}_{3}(\text{aq})

Which product is the conjugate acid of the base?

OH − \text{OH}^{-} accepts a proton to become H 2 O \text{H}_{2}\text{O}. In the reverse reaction H 2 O \text{H}_{2}\text{O} donates a proton to become OH − \text{OH}^{-}. Therefore H 2 O \text{H}_{2}\text{O} is the conjugate acid of OH − \text{OH}^{-}.

H 2 O ( ℓ ) \text{H}_{2}\text{O}(\text{ℓ}) → \to OH − ( aq ) + H + ( aq ) \text{OH}^{-}(\text{aq}) + \text{H}^{+}(\text{aq})

Write the conjugate base for:

H 2 CO 3 \text{H}_{2}\text{CO}_{3}

This is an acid. A proton needs to be donated to form the conjugate base:

H 2 CO 3 \text{H}_{2}\text{CO}_{3} → \to HCO 3 − + H + \text{HCO}_{3}^{-} + \text{H}^{+}

HCO 3 − \text{HCO}_{3}^{-} is the conjugate base of H 2 CO 3 \text{H}_{2}\text{CO}_{3}.

H 3 O + \text{H}_{3}\text{O}^{+} → \to H 2 O + H + \text{H}_{2}\text{O} + \text{H}^{+}

H 2 O \text{H}_{2}\text{O} is the conjugate base of H 3 O + \text{H}_{3}\text{O}^{+}.
