Conjugate acid-base pairs

Look at the reaction between hydrochloric acid and ammonia to form ammonium and chloride ions:

CLICKME\text{HCl}(\text{aq}) + \text{NH}_{3}(\text{g})CLICKME⇋CLICKME\text{NH}_{4}^{+}(\text{aq}) + \text{Cl}^{-}(\text{aq})

Looking at the forward reaction (i.e. the reaction that proceeds from left to right), the changes that take place can be shown as follows:

  • CLICKME{\textcolor{red}{\text{HCl(aq)}}} \to {\text{Cl}}^{-}{\text{(aq)}} + {\textcolor{darkgreen}{{\text{H}}^{+}{\text{(aq)}}}}

  • CLICKME{\textcolor{blue}{\text{NH}_{3}{\text{(g)}}}} + {\textcolor{darkgreen}{\text{H}^{+}{\text{(aq)}}}} \to {\text{NH}_{4}^{+}{\text{(aq)}}}

  • In the forward reaction, HCl is a proton donor (CLICKME{\textcolor{red}{\textbf{acid}}}) and CLICKME\text{NH}_{3} is a proton acceptor (CLICKME{\textcolor{blue}{\textbf{base}}}).

Looking at the reverse reaction (i.e. the reaction that proceeds from right to left), the changes that take place are as follows:

  • CLICKME{\textcolor{blue}{\text{Cl}^{-}{\text{(aq)}}}} + {\textcolor{darkgreen}{{\text{H}}^{+}{\text{(aq)}}}} \to {\text{HCl(aq)}}

  • CLICKME{\textcolor{red}{\text{NH}_{4}^{+}{\text{(aq)}}}} \to {\text{NH}_{3}{\text{(g)}}} + {\textcolor{darkgreen}{{\text{H}}^{+}{\text{(aq)}}}}

  • In the reverse reaction, the chloride ion (CLICKME\text{Cl}^{-}) is the proton acceptor (CLICKME{\textcolor{blue}{\textbf{base}}}) and the ammonium ion (CLICKME\text{NH}_{4}^{+}) is the proton donor (CLICKME{\textcolor{red}{\textbf{acid}}}).

In the forward reaction CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\text{HCl}} donates a proton (CLICKME\text{H}^{+}) to form CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\text{Cl}^{-}}. In the reverse reaction CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\text{Cl}^{-}} accepts a proton to form CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\text{HCl}}. CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\text{Cl}^{-}} is the CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\text{conjugate base}} of the CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\text{acid HCl}}. So CLICKME\text{HCl} and CLICKME\text{Cl}^{-} are a CLICKME\newrgbcolor{orange}{1 0.27 0}{\textcolor{orange}{\textbf{conjugate acid-base pair}}}.

Similarly, in the forward reaction CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\text{NH}_{3}} accepts a proton (CLICKME\text{H}^{+}) to form CLICKME\textcolor{red}{{\text{NH}}_{4}^{+}}. In the reverse reaction CLICKME\textcolor{red}{{\text{NH}}_{4}^{+}} donates a proton to form CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\text{NH}_{3}}. CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\text{NH}_{4}^{+}} is the CLICKME\textcolor{red}{\text{conjugate acid}} of the CLICKME\textcolor{blue}{\text{base NH}_{3}}. Which reactant is an acid and which is a base?

CLICKME\text{HNO}_{3} is nitric acid. It donates a proton in the forward reaction:

CLICKME\text{HNO}_{3}(\text{aq})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{NO}_{3}^{-}(\text{aq}) + \text{H}^{+}(\text{aq})

CLICKME\text{OH}^{-} accepts a proton in the forward reaction and is therefore the base:

CLICKME\text{OH}^{-}(\text{aq}) + \text{H}^{+}(\text{aq})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{H}_{2}\text{O}(\text{ℓ})

Which product is the conjugate base of the acid?

Nitric acid (CLICKME\text{HNO}_{3}) donates a proton to become CLICKME\text{NO}_{3}^{-}. In the reverse reaction CLICKME\text{NO}_{3}^{-} accepts a proton to become CLICKME\text{HNO}_{3}. Therefore CLICKME\text{NO}_{3}^{-} is the conjugate base of CLICKME\text{HNO}_{3}.

CLICKME\text{NO}_{3}^{-}(\text{aq}) + \text{H}^{+}(\text{aq})CLICKME\toCLICKME\text{HNO}_{3}(\text{aq})

CLICKME\text{OH}^{-} accepts a proton to become CLICKME\text{H}_{2}\text{O}. In the reverse reaction CLICKME\text{H}_{2}\text{O} donates a proton to become CLICKME\text{OH}^{-}. Therefore CLICKME\text{H}_{2}\text{O} is the conjugate acid of CLICKME\text{OH}^{-}.
