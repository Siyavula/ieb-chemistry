Volume relationships in gaseous reactions

Using what we have learnt about stoichiometry and about gases we can now apply these principles to reactions involving gases.

We will use explosions as an example.

Ammonium nitrate is used as an explosive in mining. The following reaction occurs when ammonium nitrate is heated:

2 NH 4 NO 3 (s) → 2 N 2 (g) + 4 H 2 O (g) + O 2 (g) 2\textrm{NH}_{4}\textrm{NO}_{3}\textrm{(s)} → 2\textrm{N}_{2}\textrm{(g)} + 4\textrm{H}_{2}\textrm{O (g)} + \textrm{O}_{2} \textrm{(g)}

If 750 \text{750}~ g \text{g} of ammonium nitrate is used, what volume of oxygen gas would we expect to produce (at STP)?

Work out the number of moles of ammonium nitrate

The number of moles of ammonium nitrate used is: n = m M = 750 80 = 9,375  mol \begin{align*} n &= \frac{m}{M}\\ & = \frac{750}{80}\\ & = \text{9,375}\text{ mol} \end{align*}

Work out the amount of oxygen

The mole ratio of NH 4 NO 3 \text{NH}_{4}\text{NO}_{3} to O 2 \text{O}_{2} is 2 : 1 2:1. So the number of moles of O 2 \text{O}_{2} is: n O 2 = n NH 4 NO 3 × stoichiometric coefficient O 2 stoichiometric coefficient NH 4 NO 3 = 9,375  mol NH 4 NO 3 × 1  mol O 2 2  mol  NH 4 NO 3 = 4,6875  mol \begin{align*} n_{\textrm{O}_{2}} & = n_{\textrm{NH}_{4}\textrm{NO}_{3}} \times \frac{\textrm{stoichiometric coefficient O}_{2}}{\textrm{stoichiometric coefficient NH}_{4}\textrm{NO}_{3}}\\ & = \text{9,375}\text{ mol} \textrm{NH}_{4}\textrm{NO}_{3} \times \frac{\text{1}\text{ mol} \textrm{O}_{2}}{\text{2}\text{ mol} \textrm{ NH}_{4}\textrm{NO}_{3}}\\ & = \text{4,6875}\text{ mol} \end{align*}

Work out the volume of oxygen

Recall from earlier in the chapter that we said that one mole of any gas occupies 22,4 \text{22,4}~ dm^{3} \text{dm^{3}} at STP.

V = ( 22,4 ) n = ( 22,4 ) ( 4,6875 ) = 105  dm^{3} \begin{align*} V & = (\text{22,4})n\\ & = (\text{22,4})(\text{4,6875})\\ & = \text{105}\text{ dm^{3}} \end{align*}

Airbags in cars use a controlled explosion to inflate the bag. When a car hits another car or an object, various sensors trigger the airbag. A chemical reaction then produces a large volume of gas which inflates the airbag.

Sodium azide is sometimes used in airbags. When triggered, it has the following reaction:

2 NaN 3 (s) → 2 Na(s) + 3 N 2 (g) 2\textrm{NaN}_{3}\textrm{(s)} → 2\textrm{Na(s)} + 3\textrm{N}_{2}\textrm{(g)}

If 55 \text{55} grams of sodium azide is used, what volume of nitrogen gas would we expect to produce?

Work out the number of moles of sodium azide

The number of moles of sodium azide used is: n = m M = 55 65 = 0,85  mol \begin{align*} n &= \frac{m}{M}\\ & = \frac{\text{55}}{\text{65}}\\ & = \text{0,85}\text{ mol} \end{align*}

Work out the amount of nitrogen

The mole ratio of NaN 3 \text{NaN}_{3} to N 2 \text{N}_{2} is 2 : 3 2:3. So the number of moles of N 2 \text{N}_{2} is: n N 2 = n NaN 3 × stoichiometric coefficient N 2 stoichiometric coefficient NaN 3 = 0,85  mol NaN 3 × 3  mol N 2 2  mol  NaN 3 = 1,27  mol  N 2 \begin{align*} n_{\textrm{N}_{2}} & = n_{\textrm{NaN}_{3}} \times \frac{\textrm{stoichiometric coefficient N}_{2}}{\textrm{stoichiometric coefficient NaN}_{3}}\\ & = \text{0,85}\text{ mol} \textrm{NaN}_{3} \times \frac{\text{3}\text{ mol} \textrm{N}_{2}}{\text{2}\text{ mol} \textrm{ NaN}_{3}}\\ & = \text{1,27}\text{ mol} \textrm{ N}_{2} \end{align*}

Work out the volume of nitrogen

V = ( 22,4 ) n = ( 22,4 ) ( 1,27 ) = 28,4  dm^{3} \begin{align*} V & = (\text{22,4})n\\ & = (\text{22,4})(\text{1,27})\\ & = \text{28,4}\text{ dm^{3}} \end{align*}
