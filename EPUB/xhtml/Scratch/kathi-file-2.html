Quantitative chemistry

Introduction


Balanced chemical equations

  • Know the name and formula of the following polyatomic ions: ammonium, chlorate, ethanoate, hydroxide, nitrate, nitrite, permanganate, hydrogen carbonate, hydrogen sulphate, carbonate, dichromate, sulphate, sulphite, phosphate.

  • Write chemical formulae using the periodic table and knowledge of polyatomic ions.

  • Represent chemical changes using balanced reaction equations i.e. translate word equations into chemical equations with formulae, using subscripts to represent phases (s), (l), (g) and (aq).

  • Balance reaction equations by inspection.

  • Interpret balanced reaction equations in terms of:

    • conservation of atoms

    • conservation of mass (use relative atomic masses)

The mole concept

The conservation of atoms in chemical reactions leads to the principle of conservation of matter and the ability to calculate the mass of products and reactants.

  • Describe the mole as the SI unit for amount of substance.

  • Relate amount of substance to relative atomic mass.

  • State that one mole contains Avogadro's number of particles ( N A \text{N}_{\text{A}} = 6,02 × 10 23 \text{6,02} \times \text{10}^{\text{23}}~ mol^{-1} \text{mol^{-1}}).

  • Define molar mass as as the mass in grams of one mole of that substance.

  • Calculate the molar mass of a substance given its formula.

  • Solve problems using the equation: n = m M \text{n}=\dfrac{\text{m}}{\text{M}}

    Where n = number of moles, m = mass of substance, and M = molar mass.

Molar volume of gases

  • Know and use the fact that 1 \text{1} mole of gas occupies 22,4 \text{22,4}~ dm^{3} \text{dm^{3}} at 0 \text{0} °C \text{°C} ( 273 \text{273}~ K \text{K}) and at 1 \text{1} atmosphere ( 101,3 \text{101,3}~ kPa \text{kPa}).

  • Solve problems using V = nV m \text{V} = \text{nV}_{\text{m}} where V m \text{V}_{\text{m}} = 22,4 \text{22,4}~ dm^{3} \text{dm^{3}} is the molar volume.

Concentration of solutions

  • Define solute as the substance that is dissolved in the solution.

  • Define solvent as the substance in which another substance is dissolved, forming a solution.

  • Identify the solute and/or solvent for a particular solution.

  • Define concentration as as the number of moles of solute per unit volume of solution.

  • Calculate molar concentration of a solution using the equation C = n V \text{C} = \dfrac{\text{n}}{\text{V}}

  • Define a standard solution as as a solution of known concentration.

  • Describe quantitatively and qualitatively how to make up a standard solution.

Stoichiometric calculations

  • Perform stoichiometric calculations using balanced equations (may include limiting reagents).

  • Perform stoichiometric calculations to determine the percentage yield of a chemical reaction.
