        Polarity and Covalent Bonds

        Have you seen a thin stream of water bend around a plastic ruler which has been charged by rubbing? Why does this happen? Does the water comprise of charged particles? We can test this possibility by using a conductivity meter or by attempting to electrolyse the water. The results indicate that there are too few ions to explain the pronounced deflection obtained. Perhaps all streams of liquid will behave in the same way as water! This idea can be tested by placing various liquids separately in burettes and allowing a thin stream to run into a beaker. A charged rod brought close to the stream will produce the evidence needed to answer the question. Why do some liquid streams bend towards a charged rod but not others? Has it something to do with the charge distribution in the molecules of the substance? We need to look for evidence. The analysis of an X-ray pattern it is possible to deduce the pattern of electrons in a solid. The electron pattern is usually presented as an electron density map. The results obtained compare well with computer generated pictures based on theoretical considerations.
